## ⚠️ Constraint tension (decide before producing)

[[Streams]] sets three content constraints. Two are satisfied; one conflicts:

| Constraint | Status |
|---|---|
| No face | ✅ Satisfiable — screen capture, written threads, carousels, AI-voice or text-only |
| No voice | ✅ Satisfiable — same |
| Views/algorithm-based income | ✅ Fits — YouTube/X/short-form |
| **Not trading-related** | ⚠️ **Conflict** — the sharpest edge *is* trading-flavoured |

This is a genuine decision, not mine to silently make. Three framings:

- **Framing A — Pure AI-tooling (constraint-safe).** "Building a personal AI OS / second brain." Trading is *one* demo among many (vault automation, research pipeline, agents). Honors "not trading-related"; weakest differentiation (most crowded angle).
- **Framing B — Trading-forward (sharpest, riskiest).** "I built an AI that trades real money." Strongest edge; **violates "not trading-related"**, and in India invites SEBI/advisory perception + "give me tips" liability if it looks like signals.
- **Framing C — AI-engineering, trading as case study (recommended).** Lead is *engineering a personal AI system*; the trading system is the recurring **high-credibility proof**, framed strictly as *systems/discipline*, never as tradeable signals. Threads the needle: broad AI-tooling audience, mostly honors the constraint (subject is engineering, not trading advice), keeps the edge, sidesteps advisory liability.

**Recommendation: Framing C.** It's the only one that keeps the differentiator without becoming a finance-tips channel. Requires a one-line update to the Streams "not trading-related" constraint → "not trading-*advice*; the trading *system* is fair game as engineering proof." **Confirm before publishing anything trading-flavoured.**
