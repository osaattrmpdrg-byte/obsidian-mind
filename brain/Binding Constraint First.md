# Binding Constraint First

A sharpening of [[North Star|Edge Before Action]], learned the hard way on 2026-06-16 (the EUR/USD session). Filter every money idea through this **before** investing time.

## The wrong question

> *"How do I make [the thing I'm attached to] work?"*

It anchors on the fun part (a specific instrument, signal, or tool) and silently assumes the rest will fall into place. It sends you optimizing what's already true while the link that actually decides the outcome sits unexamined.

## The right question

> [!tip] Carry this
> **"What would have to be *all* true for this to make money — and which of those is currently false?"**
> Enumerate every necessary condition up front, find the **binding constraint** (the false one), and prove *that* before touching the rest. Start at the wall, not the part you like.

## The necessary-condition chain (trading)

All must hold *simultaneously* — a chain is only as strong as its weakest link:

1. **Edge** — provable positive expectancy that survives out-of-sample, costs, and tail-stress. (State it in one sentence or it isn't found yet.)
2. **Vehicle** — an instrument that is *legal for you* **AND** *liquid enough* to capture the edge. (Legal ≠ tradeable — see [[FEMA Forex Legality]].)
3. **Execution** — you can place the order and fill near the assumed price.
4. **Survival** — sizing + drawdown tolerance to outlast the variance until the edge pays.
5. **Worth-it** — the return beats your *other* uses of the same time and capital (opportunity cost across [[Streams]]).

## The worked example that taught it

| Asset | Edge | Vehicle | Execution | Binding constraint |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EUR/USD** | ✅ | ❌ | — | Vehicle — **unfixable** (liquidity offshore-and-illegal / onshore-and-thin). Walk away. |
| **BTC** | ✅ robust | ✅ | ❌ | Execution — the CoinDCX 400, likely a **one-line fix**. |

A whole session was spent optimizing EUR/USD's *edge* (already true) and hunting vehicles/strategies/tools, when one up-front question — *"is there a legal, liquid EUR/USD vehicle?"* — would have collapsed the detour to one afternoon. The asymmetry is the lesson: EUR/USD's broken link can't be repaired no matter how clever you are; BTC's broken link is a config change. **Same effort, opposite payoff.**

## How to apply it

- Before any new idea: write the chain, mark each link ✅/❌, work **only** the ❌ that's hardest to satisfy.
- If the binding constraint is structurally unfixable (regulation, no liquidity), **stop** — no strategy/tool/vehicle swap rescues it. Redirect the energy.
- A signal-edge you can't execute is **not** an edge. "Edge" means the full tradeable chain, not the backtest.
